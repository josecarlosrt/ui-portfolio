# Transmundo: Redesigning the Path from "Where Should I Go?" to "Booked"

**Designed and built by me, from UX audit and wireframes in Figma to a production WordPress build.**

## The Problem

Transmundo, a travel agency based in Honduras, was preparing for a relaunch, but their existing website was working against them. Visitors arrived looking for travel solutions and instead landed on a site organized around the company's internal structure — directories and departmental content overshadowed the one thing users actually came to do: plan a trip.

The business goal was clear: **modernize the experience and improve conversion rates**. The UX challenge underneath it: rebuild the site around the traveler's journey instead of the company's org chart, and integrate an **AI-powered quick form** that makes requesting a trip feel effortless rather than like paperwork.

## UX Audit of the Legacy Site

Before designing anything, I ran a heuristic evaluation of the existing site. Three failure patterns stood out:

1. **Inverted visual hierarchy.** The most prominent elements on the page served internal company needs, while the primary user goal — finding travel options — was buried. Users had to work to find the product.
2. **High cognitive load and dead ends.** Navigation paths frequently led to pages with no clear next step. There was no consistent funnel from "I'm curious about a destination" to "I've contacted the agency."
3. **A rigid, desktop-first layout.** The fixed layout broke down on small screens, effectively alienating mobile users — the majority of the audience for travel inspiration browsing.

![Transmundo Audit](/images/projects/tm-1.webp)

These findings became the checklist the redesign had to answer for.

## Translating Stakeholder Vision into Design Principles

The client gave me broad creative freedom with a few concrete references: they gravitated toward Avianca's use of rounded cards and blur effects, a card-style hero concept from a Dribbble shot, their signature "blocks of blue" brand color, and a strong desire to make their active Instagram presence part of the experience.

Rather than applying these as surface styling, I translated each preference into a functional design decision:

- **Rounded cards and blur** became the core content unit of the system — destination cards, offer cards, and the hero itself — giving the site a consistent, scannable rhythm instead of decorative one-offs.
- **The card-style hero** doubles as an entry point into the trip-planning funnel, not just a visual moment: attention lands there first, and the primary CTA lives inside it.
- **"Blocks of blue"** were systematized into a defined palette with usage rules, so brand color reads as intentional structure across pages rather than scattered accents.
- **Instagram integration** was placed as social proof within the conversion flow — real trips, real travelers — reinforcing trust exactly where users hesitate.

## UX Strategy & Information Architecture

The redesigned IA funnels users along a single, intentional path: **discover → explore → plan → contact.** Every page answers "what's my next step?" — directly eliminating the dead ends found in the audit. A minimalist layout with deliberate white space keeps cognitive load low and lets the brand's blue system carry visual identity without competing with content.

The centerpiece of the funnel is the **AI-powered quick form**: instead of a long, intimidating request form, users answer a short, conversational set of inputs to start planning a trip. The goal was to collapse the distance between "browsing" and "talking to the agency" into under a minute.

## Performance and Findability as UX

Two pillars of the build treated technical quality as part of the user experience, not an afterthought:

- **Performance as UX.** I prioritized Core Web Vitals from the wireframe stage — image strategy, layout stability, and load behavior were design decisions, not post-launch fixes.
- **Search synergy.** The page structure was designed hand-in-hand with technical SEO and answer-engine optimization (AEO) — schema markup, sitemaps, and heading structures built to capture high-intent searches like "agencia de viajes Honduras."

This is where designing and building as the same person pays off: the wireframes were drawn with real implementation constraints in mind, so nothing was lost in handoff — because there was no handoff.

## Outcome

The redesigned site is live on staging and moving toward launch: a mobile-first, conversion-focused platform where the traveler's goal is the visual and structural priority on every page.
