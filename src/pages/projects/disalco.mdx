# DISALCO: Rapid Brand Identity & System Design

## Context
DISALCO, an emerging distribution business based in El Salvador, needed to establish a strong digital footprint to capture new B2B opportunities. The challenge was significant: they only had a logo, required a complete brand identity expansion, and operated under a highly aggressive timeline. They needed a functional design system and a complete website wireframe ready for immediate development.

## Goals
- **Rapid Brand Expansion:** Extract foundational brand guidelines from a single logo to create a cohesive visual identity, including typography, color palettes, and UI components.
- **Builder-Ready Design System:** Develop a structured UI kit in Figma that strictly adhered to Elementor's global styling guidelines to ensure a frictionless developer hand-off.
- **High-Fidelity Wireframing:** Deliver a strategic layout that mapped out the user journey and conversion funnels, ready for rapid deployment.

## Process
To meet the urgent deadline without compromising quality, I executed a highly focused, two-phase approach:

### 1. The 48-Hour Design System
Starting with just the client's logo, I reverse-engineered a scalable brand identity. I established a typography scale optimized for readability and defined color tokens (primary, secondary, semantic, and neutral variables). Because the development phase required speed, I structured the Figma components to mirror global site settings (e.g., Global Colors and Global Fonts in Elementor). This meant creating button states, form fields, and card layouts that could be universally applied across the site framework.

![Distribuidora Comercial UI Kit and Design System](/images/projects/disalco-1.webp)

### 2. Strategic Wireframing
With the visual rules locked in, I moved to structural wireframing. The focus was on B2B usability: clear navigation, prominent contact points, and structured product/service showcases. Instead of spending time on complex bespoke layouts, I utilized established UX patterns that prioritize fast loading times and clear conversion paths, ensuring the client could launch their initial web presence immediately.

![Distribuidora Comercial Homepage Wireframe](/images/projects/disalco-wf.webp)

## Outcome
The project culminated in the successful hand-off of a comprehensive Figma design system and complete page wireframes. By translating a single graphic asset into a fully documented UI system, I enabled the client to accelerate their development timeline drastically. Ownership of the finalized assets was transferred to the client, providing them with the exact blueprint needed to launch their digital operations in El Salvador effectively.
